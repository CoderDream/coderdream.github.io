Grammar explanation-语法解释

Relative clauses give us information about the person or thing mentioned. 关系从句为我们提供有关所提到的人或事物的信息。

Defining relative clauses give us essential information – information that tells us who or what we are talking about.定义关系从句为我们提供了基本信息——告诉我们正在谈论的人或事的信息。

The woman who lives next door works in a bank. 住在隔壁的那位女士在银行工作。
These are the flights that have been cancelled. 这些是已取消的航班。

We usually use a relative pronoun or adverb to start a defining relative clause: who, which, that, when, where or whose. 我们通常使用关系代词或副词来开始定义关系从句：who、which、that、when、where 或 who。

who/that

We can use who or that to talk about people. that is more common and a bit more informal.我们可以用 who 或 that 来谈论人。that 更常见，也更非正式。

She’s the woman who cuts my hair. 她就是那个给我剪头发的女人。
He’s the man that I met at the conference. 他就是我在会议上认识的那个人。

which/that

We can use which or that to talk about things. that is more common and a bit more informal. 我们可以用which或that来谈论事物。that 更常见，也更非正式。

There was a one-year guarantee which came with the TV. 电视附带一年保修。
The laptop that I bought last week has started making a strange noise! 我上周买的笔记本电脑开始发出奇怪的声音！

Other pronouns-其他代词

when can refer to a time. when 可以指时间。

Summer is the season when I’m happiest. 夏天是我最快乐的季节。

where can refer to a place. where 可以指一个地方。

That’s the stadium where Real Madrid play. 那是皇家马德里队比赛的体育场。

whose refers to the person that something belongs to. whose 指某物所属的人。

He’s a musician whose albums have sold millions. 他是一位专辑销量数百万张的音乐家。

Omitting the relative pronoun-省略关系代词

Sometimes we can leave out the relative pronoun. For example, we can usually leave out who, which or that if it is followed by a subject. 有时我们可以省略关系代词。例如，如果后面跟主语，我们通常可以省略 who、which 或 that。

The assistant [that] we met was really kind. 我们遇到的助理非常友善。
(we = subject, can omit that)（我们 = 主语，可以省略）

We can’t usually leave it out if it is followed by a verb. 如果后面跟动词，我们通常不能省略它。

The assistant that helped us was really kind. 帮助我们的助理真的很友善。
(helped = verb, can’t omit that)（帮助=动词，不能省略）
