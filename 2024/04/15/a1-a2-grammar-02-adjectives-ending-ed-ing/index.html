Grammar explanation语法解释

Adjectives that end in -ed (e.g. bored, interested) and adjectives that end in -ing (e.g. boring, interesting) are often confused.以 -ed 结尾的形容词（例如无聊、感兴趣）和以 -ing 结尾的形容词（例如无聊、有趣）经常会混淆。

-ed adjectives-ed 形容词

Adjectives that end in -ed generally describe emotions – they tell us how people feel.以 -ed 结尾的形容词通常描述情感——它们告诉我们人们的感受。

I was so bored in that lesson, I almost fell asleep.那堂课我实在是太无聊了，差点就睡着了。
He was surprised to see Helen after all those years.这么多年后他再次见到海伦，他感到很惊讶。
She was really tired and went to bed early.她实在是太累了，早早就睡觉了。

-ing adjectives-ing 形容词

Adjectives that end in -ing generally describe the thing that causes the emotion – a boring lesson makes you feel bored.以-ing结尾的形容词通常描述引起情绪的事物——无聊的课程让你感到无聊。

Have you seen that film? It’s really frightening.你看过那部电影吗？实在是太可怕了。
I could listen to her for hours. She’s so interesting.我可以听她讲几个小时。她真有趣。
I can’t sleep! That noise is really annoying!我睡不着！这噪音实在是太烦人了！

Here are some adjectives that can have both an -ed and an -ing form.以下是一些可以同时具有 -ed 和 -ing 形式的形容词。

annoyed生气 annoying恼人的
bored无聊的 boring无聊的
confused使困惑 confusing令人困惑的
disappointed失望的 disappointing令人失望的
excited兴奋的 exciting令人兴奋的
frightened受惊 frightening令人恐惧的
interested感兴趣的 interesting有趣的
surprised惊讶 surprising奇怪
tired疲劳的 tiring累
worried担心 worrying令人担忧
