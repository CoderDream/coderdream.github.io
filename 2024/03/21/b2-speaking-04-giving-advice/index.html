【B2-Speaking】04 Giving advice-提建议

In this video, Paul and Bob try to help Noelia with a problem. Listen to the language they use for giving advice and practise saying the useful phrases.在这段视频中，保罗和鲍勃试图帮助诺埃莉亚解决问题。聆听他们用于提供建议的语言并练习说出有用的短语。

Do the preparation exercise first. Then watch the video and do the exercises to check your understanding and practise the language.先做准备练习。然后观看视频并做练习以检查您对语言的理解和练习。

Preparation-准备

Responding to news – preparation

Put the phrases in the correct groups

1.Responding to good news
2.Responding to bad news

Transcript-对话脚本
English


Ana: Hi! I’m Ana. Welcome to What to Say! 


Do you know what to say when you respond to news? Listen out for useful language for responding to news. Then, we’ll practise saying the new phrases – after this.


Noelia: Yes, yes, yes. Ah, I did it! 


Yuna: Good news, then? 


Noelia: Yes! I won the award!


Yuna: Oh wow! Congratulations! 


Noelia: Thank you! It’s so exciting. I can’t believe it! 


Yuna: I know! That’s wonderful! Well done, Noelia. I’m really pleased for you. 


Noelia: WHAT? You cannot be serious!


Yuna: Oh dear. What’s happened? 


Noelia: They sent me the wrong email. I didn’t win. Someone else won.


Yuna: Oh no, that’s awful!


Noelia: Yes. It. Is.


Yuna: I’m so sorry, Noelia. What a shame. Can I do anything to help?


Noelia: Please pass me that magazine. Thanks.


Ana: Hello again! Oh, poor Noelia! That’s really disappointing. So, did you notice the useful phrases used for responding to news? Listen to me and then repeat. 


Oh wow! Congratulations!


I can’t believe it.


That’s wonderful!


Well done. I’m really pleased for you.


Oh dear. What’s happened?


Oh no, that’s awful.


I’m so sorry.


What a shame.


Can I do anything to help? 


Ana: Try and use some of these phrases the next time you respond to news in English. Bye for now!




English-Chinese


Ana: Hi! I’m Ana. Welcome to What to Say! 安娜：嗨！我是安娜。欢迎来到说什么！


Do you know what to say when you give advice? Listen out for useful language for giving advice. Then, we’ll practise saying the new phrases – after this.当你提供建议时你知道该说什么吗？倾听有用的语言来提供建议。然后，我们将练习说新短语——在此之后。


Noelia: Argh, this is just ridiculous! 诺莉亚：啊，这太荒谬了！


Paul: Susan again? 保罗：又是苏珊吗？


Noelia: Yeah, Susan. She just keeps emailing me about things that have nothing to do with work and nothing to do with me. What do you think I should do? 诺莉亚：是的，苏珊。她只是不断地给我发电子邮件，谈论一些与工作无关、与我无关的事情。你觉得我应该怎么做？


Paul: Well, if I were you, I’d email and ask her to stop sending them.保罗：好吧，如果我是你，我会发电子邮件让她停止发送。


Noelia: I’m not sure that’s a good idea. I have to be careful what I say as she’s quite an important client. 诺莉亚：我不确定这是个好主意。我必须小心我所说的话，因为她是一位非常重要的客户。


Paul: Erm, I’m not sure then. Perhaps you could try talking to Yuna about it?保罗：嗯，那我不确定。也许你可以尝试和尤娜谈谈这件事？


Bob: Er .. why don’t you try doing nothing?鲍勃：呃..你为什么不尝试什么都不做呢？


Noelia: Sorry? 诺莉亚：抱歉？


Bob: Perhaps you could just … do nothing? 鲍勃：也许你可以……什么也不做？


Noelia: Really? I’m not too sure about that.诺莉亚：真的吗？我对此不太确定。


Bob: Well, I remember this guy I used to work with, Mark, and he kept emailing again and again and again, about all sorts, mostly unrelated to work. Well, I kept responding politely and he kept sending them. In the end, I stopped replying to his emails unless they were only about work. 鲍勃：嗯，我记得曾经和我一起工作的那个人，马克，他不断地一次又一次地发电子邮件，内容涉及各种各样的事情，大部分与工作无关。好吧，我一直有礼貌地回复，他也一直发送。最后，我不再回复他的电子邮件，除非它们只是与工作有关。


Paul: And what happened? 保罗：发生了什么事？


Bob: He stopped sending them. It’s a bit like being faced with a dangerous snake. If you move quickly, it’ll bite you, but if you keep very still and do nothing, eventually it’ll move away and leave you alone. I had to learn the hard way. 鲍勃：他不再发送它们了。这有点像面对一条危险的蛇。如果你移动得很快，它就会咬你，但如果你保持静止不动，什么也不做，它最终会走开，留下你一个人。我必须以艰难的方式学习。


Noelia: Hey, Bob. Have you got a minute?诺莉亚：嘿，鲍勃。你有时间吗？


Bob: Yeah.鲍勃：是的。


Noelia: So, I tried what you suggested and I only responded to Susan’s emails about work for a few days. Then guess what?诺埃莉亚：所以，我尝试了你的建议，但我只回复了苏珊关于工作的电子邮件几天。那你猜怎么着？


Bob: The irrelevant emails stopped. 鲍勃：不相关的电子邮件停止了。


Noelia: Yes! Now she only sends me work-related ones.诺埃利亚：是的！现在她只给我发与工作相关的邮件。


Bob: Well, that’s very good news.鲍勃：嗯，这是个好消息。


Noelia: All thanks to you, Bob.诺莉亚：这一切都谢谢你，鲍勃。


Bob: Any time, Noelia, any time! All in a day’s work. Ahh! 鲍勃：任何时候，诺莉亚，任何时候！一切都在一天的工作中。啊！


Ana: Hello again! It looks like Bob is in Noelia’s good books. So, did you notice the useful phrases used for giving advice? Listen to me and then repeat. 安娜：你好！看起来鲍勃在诺埃莉亚的好书中。那么，您注意到用于提供建议的有用短语了吗？听我说，然后重复。


What do you think I should do?你觉得我应该怎么做？


If I were you, I’d ask her**.**如果我是你，我就会问她。


I’m not sure that’s a good idea.我不确定这是个好主意。


Perhaps you could try talking to Yuna**.**也许你可以尝试和尤娜谈谈。


Why don’t you try doing nothing**?**你为什么不尝试什么都不做呢？


I’m not too sure about that.我对此不太确定。


I tried what you suggested. 我尝试了你的建议。


Ana: Try and use some of these phrases the next time you give advice in English. Bye for now! 安娜：下次你用英语提供建议时，尝试使用其中一些短语。暂时再见！
